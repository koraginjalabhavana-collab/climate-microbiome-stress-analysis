# Results: Climate Stress Effects on Coral and Soil Microbiomes

## 1. Alpha Diversity – Shannon Index

### Coral
- **Observation**: Diversity is lower during the bleaching peak (August 2020) and increases in later months (recovery).  
- **Interpretation**: Thermal stress temporarily reduces species evenness/richness, but the community shows resilience.  
- **File**: `figures/coral_shannon.png`

### Soil
- **Observation**: No clear difference between control and stress groups (drought + future warming).  
- **Interpretation**: Soil microbial communities appear more resistant to the combined stress, possibly due to functional redundancy.  
- **File**: `figures/soil_shannon.png`

## 2. Beta Diversity – Community Composition

### Coral (PCoA, Bray‑Curtis)
- **Variance explained**: PCoA1 = 9.2%, PCoA2 = 8.0%  
- **Pattern**: Clear separation between bleaching and recovery groups (ellipses overlap minimally).  
- **Conclusion**: Thermal stress drives a substantial shift in community composition.  
- **File**: `figures/coral_pcoa.png`

### Soil (PCA, Hellinger‑transformed)
- **Variance explained**: PC1 = 13.3%, PC2 = 9.7%  
- **Pattern**: Partial separation between control and stress groups, with overlapping ellipses.  
- **Conclusion**: Stress also alters soil community composition, though the effect is less pronounced than in coral.  
- **File**: `figures/soil_pca.png`

## 3. Microbial Interaction Networks

### Coral Network
- **Structure**: Dense, modular network with several highly connected hubs.  
- **Interpretation**: Suggests a stable core microbiome with strong co‑occurrence patterns; hub taxa may play keystone roles.  
- **File**: `figures/coral_network_final.png`

### Soil Network
- **Structure**: Less centralised than coral; still modular but with lower connectivity.  
- **Interpretation**: Reflects a more distributed interaction structure, likely due to the complex, variable soil environment.  
- **File**: `figures/soil_network_final.png`

## 4. Synthesis

- **Diversity**: Coral shows stress‑induced dip followed by recovery; soil diversity remains stable.  
- **Composition**: Both systems reorganise their communities under stress, but the coral response is more dramatic.  
- **Networks**: Coral networks are more centralised (host‑driven), soil networks are more distributed (environment‑driven).  

**Conclusion**: Climate stress does not simply reduce microbial diversity – it restructures communities in ecosystem‑specific ways. Coral microbiomes rely on a few keystone taxa, while soil microbiomes rely on functional redundancy. These differences reflect the contrasting ecological constraints of host‑associated vs. free‑living environments.
